# MED-Estation Project Report

MED-Estation (also represented in parts of the codebase branding as Zephyra) is an AI-powered pharmacy inventory intelligence platform designed to solve high-impact operational problems in modern pharmaceutical workflows. The system addresses counterfeit medicine risks, medicine expiry loss, stock-out events, demand volatility, and fragmented pharmacy decision-making through one integrated full-stack platform. Instead of treating inventory, prescriptions, procurement, and safety validation as isolated tasks, the project unifies them into an event-aware architecture where each operational action can trigger analytics, alerts, and recommended decisions. The result is a practical healthcare technology system built to improve medicine availability, reduce wastage, and support safer dispensing in both retail and institutional pharmacy environments.

The platform follows a full-stack architecture with a FastAPI backend and a modern web frontend built using Next.js, React, and TypeScript. On the backend, asynchronous SQLAlchemy is used for data access and transaction consistency, while configuration management is handled through environment-driven settings. The backend can run quickly with SQLite for local development and demonstrations, and the repository also includes container-ready infrastructure for PostgreSQL usage in more production-like environments. This dual setup reflects a clear design goal: rapid iteration during prototype phases while preserving a migration path toward scalable deployment. The frontend and backend are integrated through versioned API routes, and the startup scripts orchestrate both services for streamlined execution during demos and evaluations.

At the data-model level, MED-Estation is structured around core pharmacy entities such as drugs, suppliers, stock batches, sales logs, and alerts, with additional clinical entities like patients, doctors, and prescriptions. The project extends beyond traditional inventory schemas by introducing dedicated models for verified medicine batches and an exchange proposal workflow for expiry-based redistribution between participating pharmacies. A notable reliability feature is the immutable audit ledger mechanism that stores hash-linked records, enabling tamper-evident tracking of important actions. This design choice is significant for regulated healthcare contexts because it strengthens trust, accountability, and traceability in operational records.

From a workflow perspective, the inventory subsystem supports batch-centric tracking and FEFO-aligned views, helping teams prioritize dispensing from stock that expires sooner while preserving safety constraints. Prescription workflows accept digital inputs and simulated OCR-style extraction paths, then connect those entries to dispensing and sales signals used downstream by analytics services. Reporting endpoints expose dashboard metrics, trends, and operational status indicators that allow decision-makers to move from raw transaction data to actionable insights. Dataset ingestion modules process CSV and spreadsheet files in the background, allowing quick initialization and continuous updates for realistic pharmacy records without forcing manual entry of every stock event.

The intelligence layer is one of the most impactful parts of the system. FEFO services continuously assess batch expiry windows and classify stock into operational risk states such as safe, approaching expiry, critical, or expired. Forecasting services use historical sales behavior and refill dynamics to estimate future demand horizons, while predictive reorder services compare forecasted requirements against usable stock and safety buffers to generate procurement guidance. This creates an end-to-end analytical chain from transaction history to recommended action, which is especially important in high-variability medicine demand environments where static reorder thresholds often fail.

MED-Estation also incorporates an AI-assisted chatbot service grounded in live database context. Instead of relying only on generic responses, the chatbot assembles inventory and clinical context snapshots to answer operational questions and can trigger communication-oriented flows related to reordering and follow-up actions. Alongside this, the security module validates medicine authenticity by checking supplied batch details against local stock records and a verified registry. This architecture directly supports counterfeit detection workflows and strengthens confidence in medicine sourcing decisions. In parallel, exchange-network functionality introduces a practical mechanism for near-expiry inventory redistribution, turning potential waste into cross-network availability.

Technically, the platform is designed in an event-driven style using an in-process asynchronous broker and background workers. Core events such as stock updates, prescription additions, and dataset uploads are published and consumed by worker handlers to trigger recalculations, alert refreshes, and downstream analytics updates. This keeps the system responsive and modular without requiring heavy external messaging infrastructure at prototype stage. The current implementation is intentionally pragmatic for hackathon-speed delivery, while comments and structure indicate clear extensibility toward production-grade brokers and distributed patterns in future iterations.

The frontend experience mirrors the backend capabilities through role-relevant dashboard modules and feature pages focused on practical pharmacy operations. User-facing modules include inventory intelligence views, demand forecasting displays, billing and POS-aligned interfaces, chatbot interactions, supplier and procurement workflows, patient and insurance integration, counterfeit detection screens, and exchange-network oriented pages. This breadth demonstrates that the project is not only model-centric but also usability-oriented, with visual workflows that help transform advanced analytics into everyday decision support for pharmacy teams.

Overall, MED-Estation is best understood as an applied healthcare intelligence platform that connects safety, operations, and analytics into one coherent product. Its design combines strong engineering fundamentals (modular APIs, async data access, schema separation, background event handling) with practical healthcare focus areas (authenticity assurance, wastage reduction, demand prediction, auditability). The project’s value lies in this integration: each component contributes independently, but the strongest impact emerges from their combined operation as a closed decision loop from data capture to autonomous recommendations.

## Appendix A: Hackathon Build Context and Achievement

This project was built and demonstrated within the constrained timeline of **AxioML Hackathon 2026**, a two-day external inter-college innovation event hosted by **Pratyusha Engineering College** in **Tiruvallur, Tamil Nadu, India**, on **3rd March to 4th March 2026**. The event gathered more than fifty external teams from multiple institutions and around thirty internal teams from the host college, creating a highly competitive environment focused on solving real-world problems using artificial intelligence, machine learning, and modern software systems. Our team represented **Vellore Institute of Technology** and presented MED-Estation as an AI-driven pharmacy operations and intelligence platform.

During final evaluation, the project was assessed on innovation quality, technical feasibility, real-world healthcare relevance, implementation depth, and clarity of presentation. MED-Estation received strong appreciation for combining counterfeit drug detection, intelligent demand forecasting, expiry-based redistribution strategy, and autonomous pharmacy decision support into a single operational system. After final presentation and live technical demonstration, the project was awarded **First Prize** at AxioML Hackathon 2026, with a **cash prize of Rs. 10,000**. This recognition reflects the team’s ability to design and deliver an end-to-end healthcare technology solution under strict hackathon constraints while maintaining both technical depth and practical impact.
